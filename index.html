About

Hey! I'm Adam and I make custom bags generally related to bicycles out of my shack near Carrboro, North Carolina. I've been sewing bags for about 5 years now, and under the DFL (Dead Fucking Last) moniker since ~2013. I do almost exclusively one-off custom projects for folks so I normally don't have any stock bags, but that may change so check back within the decade. Things I commonly make are: frame bags, panniers, backpacks, messenger bags, tool rolls, handlebar bags, saddle bags and fanny packs, but I (usually) love making weird super custom stuff so hit me up with any questions. I don't have internet in the shack so it might take me a couple days to get back to emails, so including your phone number and times I could call makes things a bit easier.

There are photos of couple example projects further down this page, along with a bit more information on ordering. There are also more bag photos and more recent news on my instagram feed.

Also, people making their own gear is radical and I definitely relied on internet people when I was learning so email me if yer making stuff and want help with something!

AND If yer lucky enough to be bicycle touring through the beautiful NC Piedmont hit me up if ya need a shower, bike tools, or a place to crash for a night or two!

Ordering A Bag

So ya want a bag, huh? Great! let's make it happen.

First off, take a look at some of my previous work to get an idea of the stuff I make and the ballpark prices. In general the bags I make are on the heavier side (nothing crazy but I generally like double layers of good ol 1000D Cordura and almost always use nice'n beefy #10 zippers) so if you're counting grams or looking for a ultralight race bag this may not be the best fit, I can point you in the direction of some great folks making that kind of bag if ya want. That being said, I can use lighter materials (sil-nylon, vx21, etc) and construction methods upon request.

Concerning timing; it's just little old me out here so things can take a little while depending on my waitlist and if I've been off touring recently, but the wait generally ranges from 3 days to 2 months, so hit me up!

Still in? sweet! shoot me an email describing what ya want at adamhb14@gmail.com or send me a letter:

Adam
3702 Arthur Minnis Rd

I'll get back to you with a timeline and price. It's easiest if you include your phone number in the correspondence and I'll call you up to figure out details/colors/etc, sketches are also super helpful and super awesome.

My prices are all based on: $15/hour labor + material costs + shipping. On bigger projects I'll ask for half payment before I start and half before I ship just to offset material cost if someone pulls out halfway through.
